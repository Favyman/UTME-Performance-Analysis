# UTME-Performance-Analysis in Nigeria
This repo contains an end-to-end UTME Performance Analysis Project Performed in Excel and Power BI
## 📌 Project Overview

This project explores the key factors influencing the performance of secondary school students in the Unified Tertiary Matriculation Examination (UTME) in Nigeria. Using statistical analysis and interactive dashboards, the study identifies the educational, socioeconomic, and behavioral factors most associated with high and low student performance.

The project applies regression analysis, descriptive statistics, hypothesis testing, and dashboard visualization to provide data-driven recommendations for educators, policymakers, parents, and other education stakeholders.


## 🎯 Business Problem

Poor performance in the Unified Tertiary Matriculation Examination (UTME) remains a major concern in Nigeria despite continuous investments in education.

Educational stakeholders often struggle to understand which factors contribute most to student performance, making it difficult to design effective intervention programs.

This project aims to identify the major predictors of UTME performance and provide evidence-based recommendations for improving academic outcomes.


## 🎯 Objectives

- Analyze the relationship between attendance rate and UTME performance.
- Determine the effects of study habits, teacher quality, and access to learning materials on student performance.
- Compare UTME performance between students in urban and rural schools.
- Examine the influence of socioeconomic background and parental involvement.
- Identify the strongest factors associated with high and low UTME performance.

 # 📌 Key Findings

### 1. Teacher Quality is the Strongest Predictor

Students taught by highly qualified teachers consistently achieved better UTME scores than those taught by low-quality teachers.

### 2. Study Hours Improve Performance

Students who dedicate more hours to studying generally perform better in UTME.

### 3. Parental Involvement Matters

Students with actively involved parents significantly outperform those with little or no parental engagement.

### 4. Socioeconomic Status Influences Performance

Students from higher socioeconomic backgrounds tend to achieve better academic outcomes due to improved access to educational support and resources.

### 5. Attendance Has a Positive but Limited Effect

Although better attendance contributes positively to performance, it explains only a small portion of the variation in UTME scores.

### 6. School Location Has Minimal Impact

The analysis found no meaningful difference in UTME performance between students attending urban and rural schools.

# 📈 Project Outcome

This analysis demonstrates that improving UTME performance requires a multi-dimensional approach rather than focusing on a single factor.

Teacher quality, parental involvement, socioeconomic support, and effective study habits collectively play a much greater role in student success than school location alone.
- Implement early intervention programs for students with poor attendance and academic performance.
